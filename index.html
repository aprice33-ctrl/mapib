Beginner guide

Think of ratios as financial vital signs. Liquidity asks, “Can the company pay soon?” Leverage asks, “How much debt is supporting assets?” Profitability asks, “How much profit is produced?” Market ratios ask, “How much are investors willing to pay?”

What changed?

Liquidity: raising current assets usually improves the current ratio. But if inventory is a big part of current assets, the quick ratio may stay weaker because inventory may not turn into cash quickly.

Leverage: more debt can increase ROE through the equity multiplier, but it also increases risk because the company must cover interest payments.

Profitability: higher net income improves profit margin, ROA, and ROE. ROE can rise because the company is more profitable, more efficient, or more leveraged.
